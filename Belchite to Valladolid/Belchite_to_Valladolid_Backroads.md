# Belchite → Valladolid — *Strictly Backroads* Variant

**The motorway-free line across the High Plains**

This is the no-autovía version of the Belchite → Valladolid crossing: every kilometre stays on the **old N-roads and regional carreteras** — the empty, curving roads that traffic abandoned when the motorways were built. It's slower and a little longer than the fast line, but it *is* the route the guide always wanted: frontier castles, *meseta* plains, pine forest and the Ribera del Duero, with no autovía under your wheels.

Routed on OpenStreetMap data with motorways and tolls switched off (FOSSGIS Valhalla, `use_highways=0`), then verified to contain **zero autovía**.

The places are listed **in the exact order you meet them**. Numbered entries (1–10) are the fixed waypoints; lettered entries (e.g. 1a, 5b) are optional detours on or just off the connecting road.

### Roads ridden, leg by leg

| Leg | Old road(s) |
|-----|-------------|
| Belchite → Daroca | **A-222** (regional) |
| Daroca → Calatayud | **N-234** |
| Calatayud → Arcos de Jalón | **N-IIa** (old N-II) through the Jalón valley |
| Arcos → Almazán | **CL-116** |
| Almazán → El Burgo de Osma | **CL-116** (scenic Duero-frontier parallel for the detours) |
| El Burgo → San Leonardo de Yagüe | **SO-920** into the Río Lobos canyon |
| San Leonardo → Santo Domingo de Silos | **BU-910** over the Peñas de Cervera |
| Silos → Peñafiel | **BU-/VA-** regional roads + **N-122** in the Duero valley |
| Peñafiel → Valladolid | **VA-101 / N-122** Tempranillo country |

> **What changed vs the fast version:** the fast tracks took ~20% autovía (A-2 in the Jalón valley, A-11 along the Duero, A-15 near Soria) because those motorways now shadow the old roads. This variant rides the **parallel old roads** instead — e.g. the **N-IIa** beside the A-2, the **N-122** beside the A-11 — adding ~80 km but no motorway.

## The route, stop by stop

### 1 · Belchite 🟢
*Start • 41.3047, -0.7545*

The most striking stop and the place to begin. The **Pueblo Viejo** was a town of ~3,800 in August 1937; by September it was a graveyard after one of the Civil War's most famous battles (5,000+ dead). Franco left it in ruins as a "living monument" beside a new town. Considered one of Spain's most **haunted** places, with history day-tours and paranormal night-tours — **book online ahead**, no door sales, guided access only from the New Town. Film lore: Gilliam's *Baron Munchausen* and del Toro's *Pan's Labyrinth* shot here. Bring water/shoes; the **El Planerón** steppe nearby is a birding reserve (Dupont's Lark).

### 1a · Fuendetodos 🟠
*Belchite → Daroca (A-222) • 41.3433, -0.9594*

**Goya's birthplace** (1746). Visit his birth house and the **Museo del Grabado** (the *Caprichos*, *Disasters of War*, *Bullfighting*, *Follies*). A poignant pairing with Belchite. Nearby: a preserved Roman dam at Almonacid de la Cuba, the eerie hamlet of Rodén, salt-flat lagoons along the A-222.

### 2 · Daroca 🔵
*Belchite → Daroca (A-222) • 41.1144, -1.4144*

Walled medieval town in a ravine. **La Mina** — a 600 m flood-diversion tunnel (1555–60); the **Basílica de los Sagrados Corporales** with the bleeding-host miracle relics; and a quirky **three-storey pastry museum** by the 1874 Manuel Segura bakery.

### 2a · Anento 🟠
*Daroca → Calatayud (N-234) • 41.0698, -1.3340*

Among "the prettiest villages in Spain" — castle, cobbled lanes, and the **Aguallueve** dripping-spring grotto via a short pretty walk.

### 2b · Laguna de Gallocanta 🟠
*Daroca → Calatayud (N-234) • 40.9741, -1.5063*

**Western Europe's largest saltwater lagoon.** Autumn–spring, up to **60,000 migrating cranes** plus 40+ species; flamingos and ducks at other times. Edge-of-lake hides with binoculars. Unmissable in winter, atmospheric in summer at ~1,000 m.

### 3 · Calatayud 🔵
*Daroca → Calatayud (N-234) • 41.3532, -1.6468*

On Roman **Bilbilis Augusta** (poet Martial's birthplace), free ruins ~5–6 km out. A capital of Aragonese **Mudéjar** art (UNESCO towers). Oddities: the **leaning tower** of San Pedro de los Francos and the **Mesón de la Dolores** copla-legend inn; young-Goya frescoes in San Juan el Real; cave houses above.

### 3a · Medinaceli 🟠
*Calatayud → Arcos de Jalón • 41.1546, -2.4406*

A spectacular hilltop village — the **only three-span Roman triumphal arch in Spain**, on a ridge near 1,200 m over the Jalón. Celtic-Roman-Arab-Christian layers; on the Camino del Cid.

### 4 · Arcos de Jalón 🔵
*Calatayud → Arcos de Jalón • 41.1381, -2.2630*

Frontier town under a ruined hilltop castle. The treasures are near: **Santa María de Huerta** (~11 km), a great Cistercian monastery with a masterpiece 1215 refectory; the **Jalón gorges** around Somaén (birds of prey); Roman Arcóbriga near Monreal de Ariza.

### 5 · Almazán 🔵
*Arcos de Jalón → Almazán (CL-116) • 41.5129, -2.5594*

Walled town on the Duero, heart of the empty plains. Three medieval gates survive; the **Plaza Mayor** holds the Renaissance Hurtado de Mendoza palace and Romanesque **San Miguel** (eight-pointed star vault). A *postigo* leads to a Duero viewpoint.

### 5a · Berlanga de Duero 🟠
*Almazán → El Burgo de Osma (CL-116, scenic parallel) • 41.4642, -2.8623*

A 15th-century castle and late-Gothic Colegiata. Inside rest **Fray Tomás de Berlanga** (who discovered and named the Galápagos) and his **stuffed crocodile**.

### 5b · Ermita de San Baudelio 🟠
*Almazán → El Burgo de Osma (scenic parallel) • 41.4183, -2.7902*

A **Mozarabic jewel** near Casillas de Berlanga: 11th-century murals around a central pillar that fans out **like a palm tree**. One of Spain's great secret art sites. **Closed Mondays.**

### 5c · Rello 🟠
*Almazán → El Burgo de Osma (scenic parallel) • 41.3739, -2.7269*

A near-perfect tiny **walled medieval village** — gates, walls and old *rollo* intact.

### 5d · Calatañazor 🟠
*Almazán → El Burgo de Osma (scenic parallel) • 41.6992, -2.8175*

A village **frozen in time** with conical chimneys and a ruined castle; "Almanzor lost his drum" here (1002). Beside it, the ancient **Sabinar** juniper forest and the **La Fuentona** karst spring.

### 6 · El Burgo de Osma 🔵
*Almazán → El Burgo de Osma (CL-116) • 41.5876, -3.0669*

Arcaded cathedral town with a lively evening **tapas** scene; a huge Gothic-Romanesque cathedral. Nearby: **Castillo de Gormaz** (largest caliphal fortress in Europe) and rock-carved Roman **Tiermes**.

### 6a · Castillo de Ucero 🟠
*El Burgo de Osma → San Leonardo (SO-920) • 41.7191, -3.0456*

The run into the **Cañón del Río Lobos** — a Gothic-Templar castle on a crag, park interpretation centre, and the La Galiana trailhead.

### 7 · San Leonardo de Yagüe 🔵
*El Burgo de Osma → San Leonardo (SO-920) • 41.8286, -3.0877*

Cool pine country. The **Cañón del Río Lobos** — limestone cliffs, **griffon vultures**, dark skies — and its centrepiece, the Templar **Ermita de San Bartolomé** (esoteric rose window), with a Bronze-Age cave and the "Devil's Eye" arch. Easy ~2.5 km walk in.

### 7a · Desfiladero de la Yecla 🟠
*San Leonardo → Santo Domingo de Silos (BU-910) • 41.9499, -3.4423*

A **gorge barely two metres wide**, threaded by hanging walkways, with 100+ pairs of griffon vultures above. ~15–20 min round trip. Nearby: the 9th-century Mozarabic Ermita de Santa Cecilia.

### 8 · Santo Domingo de Silos 🔵
*San Leonardo → Santo Domingo de Silos (BU-910) • 41.9625, -3.4189*

The **Benedictine monastery** — its 11th-century two-storey **Romanesque cloister** is among Europe's finest. The **cypress of Silos** (1883) and the monks who hit the 1990s **pop charts** with Gregorian chant. Aim for sung **vespers ~7 pm**. **Closed Mondays.**

### 8a · Caleruega 🟠
*Santo Domingo de Silos → Peñafiel (VA-223) • 41.8253, -3.4858*

**Birthplace of Santo Domingo de Guzmán**, founder of the Dominican Order. Torreón de los Guzmanes, Dominican monastery, the region's oldest wine cellar. You're now in Ribera del Duero.

### 9 · Peñafiel 🔵
*Santo Domingo de Silos → Peñafiel (VA-223) • 41.5985, -4.1245*

The finale in wine country: a castle **shaped like a stone ship** on a ridge, now the **Provincial Wine Museum**; below, the **Plaza del Coso**, one of Spain's first main squares, with wooden Arabesque balconies and rock-cut wine cellars (Protos at the castle's foot).

### 10 · Valladolid 🔴
*End • 41.6544, -4.7223*

Journey's end — a run through Tempranillo country to the regional capital.

## Planning notes

- **Book ahead:** Belchite (day & night tours sell out online), Santo Domingo de Silos (closed Mondays; vespers ~7 pm), Ermita de San Baudelio (closed Mondays).
- **Spend spare time in central Soria** (Almazán → El Burgo de Osma) — the densest cluster of frontier castles, Mozarabic art and time-frozen villages.
- **Bookend pilgrimages:** Goya at Fuendetodos near the start, St. Dominic at Caleruega near the end.
- **GPX files (this variant):** `route_basic_backroads.gpx` (~625 km) and `route_enhanced_backroads.gpx` (~838 km) — dense tracks on OSM roads, **verified motorway-free**. The fast versions (`route_basic.gpx`, `route_enhanced.gpx`) and originals (`originals_backup/`) remain available.
- **Pace:** on old roads, reckon ~55–65 km/h moving; the enhanced backroads route is genuinely a two-day drive with the stops folded in.
