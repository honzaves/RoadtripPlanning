# Uitgeest → Belchite — Backroads Route Booklet (Redesigned)

**A four-day motorcycle route through forgotten France & over the Pyrenees into Aragón**

A reworking of the original line, built to skip the dull bits and string together the quiet, spectacular and overlooked. The boring kilometres are compressed into an easy transit Day 1; the three days that follow run through the Morvan, the wild Gorges de l'Allier, the **Gorges du Tarn**, and finish with a **scenic Pyrenean crossing through the Cerdanya** — trading the old coastal-motorway dash for a real mountain pass and the magnificent N-260.

**Legend** — 🏁 Start / End · ⛺ Campsite (overnight) · 🏨 Hotel/Hostel · 📍 Sight · 💎 *Quiet, spectacular & little-known*

> **How this differs from the original:** night 1 at **Toul** (not Verdun) for an easier start · Day 3 now rides the **Gorges du Tarn** and is entirely motorway-free · Day 4 crosses the Pyrenees over the **Cerdanya / Puigcerdà** and comes down the **N-260** through the Catalan pre-Pyrenees, instead of Le Perthus + the AP-7 coastal motorway.

> **A note on distances:** figures are planning estimates from the routed GPX, not live routing — sanity-check on your GPS. Days run ~7–9 h; Day 2 is the long one. Camping each night except Belchite (hotel).

## Day 1 · Wednesday — Uitgeest → Toul

![Day 1 route — Uitgeest to Toul](images/new_day1_uitgeest_to_toul.png)

*Motorway transit · ~557 km · ~6.8 h · A2/E25 through Belgium & Luxembourg into Lorraine*

The get-it-done day: pure autoroute south so the rest of the trip can be all good roads. Aim to roll into Toul mid–late afternoon with the evening free.

**Along the road**

- 💎 **Toul itself** — an unexpectedly handsome reward for a motorway day. A small fortified city inside intact **Vauban star-ramparts**, with a soaring Gothic **Cathédrale Saint-Étienne** and the **Collégiale Saint-Gengoult** cloister — and almost no tourists. A perfect stroll-and-dinner town for night one.

### ⛺ Overnight — camping near Toul

- **Camping de Villey-le-Sec ★★★** — the standout local choice, on the bank of the Moselle in the Haye forest between Nancy and Toul (A31 exit 15), ~8 km from Toul; on-site shop and the *Panorama* restaurant terrace.
  [campingvilleylesec.fr (official)](https://www.campingvilleylesec.fr/) · [Pitchup booking](https://www.pitchup.com/en-us/campsites/france/lorraine/meurthe_et_moselle/villey_le_sec/camping_de_villey_le_sec/)
- **ACSI / Eurocampings listing** — same site, alternative booking route with reviews.
  [eurocampings.co.uk](https://www.eurocampings.co.uk/france/lorraine/meurthe-et-moselle/villey-le-sec/camping-villey-le-sec-112527/)
- *Fallback:* campgrounds are thin right around Toul, so if Villey-le-Sec is full, Toul town has plenty of small hotels within the ramparts for a one-night stop.

## Day 2 · Thursday — Toul to Langeac

### Morvan forests & the wild Gorges de l'Allier

![Day 2 route — Toul to Langeac](images/new_day2_toul_to_langeac.png)

*~595 km · ~9 h · the long day · motorway start, then all backroads · camp on the Allier*

Open on motorway — A31 then A6 — to clear the dull Lorraine/Burgundy plains fast. Exit at **Saulieu** and the rest is D-roads: across the granite Morvan, over the Loire at Decize, then a long drop into volcanic Auvergne and the mouth of the Allier gorges. The highway head-start is what keeps this, the trip's longest day, inside your window.

**Along the road**

1. 📍 **Saulieu** — where you leave the A6 and the riding begins. Old Burgundy market town for fuel and a first coffee.
2. 💎 **Bibracte, Mont Beuvray** — a Gaulish capital on a forested 800 m summit, reached by a sublime beech-lined road; ramparts in the woods, big panoramas, an excellent museum, and hardly a soul off-season. The Morvan's hidden highlight.
3. 📍 **Saut de Gouloux** — a short forest path to a quiet waterfall, right on the D977bis — essentially no detour.
4. 📍 **Moulins-Engilbert** — a sleepy Morvan bourg with an old market square and almost no visitors.
5. 💎 **Chilhac** — a tiny village ringed by basalt columns on a spur above the Allier; goat cheese and an artisan biscuit-maker. Overlooked and genuinely peaceful.

> **Lunch:** **Lac des Settons (Morvan)** — lakeside spots for a water-view break before you drop south. Or **Decize** on the Loire, riverside cafés near the midpoint.

> **Avoid:** **Vézelay** (UNESCO tour-bus magnet, east of your line) and dropping into **Nevers** proper (ring-road traffic on an already long day).

> **Faster alternative:** if the day runs long, skip the Morvan loop and stay on the A6 to ~Mâcon/Paray-le-Monial before cutting west to the Allier — buys back close to an hour. The scenic version is the better ride; this is the safety net.

### ⛺ Overnight — camping near Langeac (on the Allier)

- **Camping des Gorges de l'Allier ★★★ — Langeac** — large wooded riverside park 5 min from town, at the mouth of the gorges; pool, chalets, gîte d'étape. ☎ +33 4 71 77 05 01.
  [campinglangeac.com (official)](https://www.campinglangeac.com/) · [Pitchup booking](https://www.pitchup.com/campsites/france/auvergne/haute-loire/langeac/camping-des-gorges-de-lallier/)
- **VVF Les Gorges de l'Allier — Lavoûte-Chilhac (~13 km)** — 3-star holiday village in one of France's prettiest villages, heated pool; chalets rather than tents.
  [campings.com listing](https://www.campings.com/en-gb/campsite/vvf-villages-les-gorges-de-l-allier-lavoute-chilhac-94167)
- **Camping Municipal de Chilhac** — small, simple riverside municipal pitch right at the basalt village (sight #5); cheap and quiet.
  [france-voyage.com area listing](https://www.france-voyage.com/campsites/lavoute-chilhac-commune-15357.htm)

## Day 3 · Friday — Langeac to Galamus

### Allier gorges, Mont Lozère & the Gorges du Tarn — the wild day

![Day 3 route — Langeac to Galamus](images/new_day3_langeac_to_galamus.png)

*~495 km · ~8.8 h · **zero motorway** · sleep at the foot of Galamus*

The purest riding of the trip — not a metre of motorway. Follow the Allier gorges, climb the lonely Mont Lozère, then drop into the **Gorges du Tarn**, one of France's great canyon roads, before crossing the Haut-Languedoc to camp right under the Gorges de Galamus. Sleeping here puts Galamus at your doorstep for a dawn ride and keeps the final day short.

**Along the road**

1. 💎 **Cave Chapel Sainte-Madeleine, Monistrol-d'Allier** — a chapel built into the rock above the Allier on the old pilgrim road; a cool, often-missed picnic spot.
2. 💎 **Cascade de Rûnes (Mont Lozère)** — an off-radar waterfall reached by a short rocky path; free, and usually empty.
3. 📍 **Florac** — gateway to the Cévennes and the Tarn; good fuel/coffee before the canyon.
4. 💎 **Gorges du Tarn — the quiet stretch** — the 10 km from **Sainte-Énimie** (a *plus beaux villages de France*) down to **La Malène** is the loveliest and most tranquil part; ride it early and it's yours.
5. 💎 **Point Sublime** — the grandest panorama over the canyon, above the Cirque des Baumes where the Tarn turns at a right angle; magical at low sun.
6. 💎 **Roc des Hourtous** — a cliff-edge viewpoint over the narrowest, wildest part of the gorge, on the Causse Méjean side — far quieter than Point Sublime.
7. 💎 **Le Sidobre** (near Lacaune/Castres) — Europe's largest **granite chaos**: rivers of giant boulders in the forest, including the **Peyro Clabado**, an ~800-tonne rock balanced on a one-square-metre base. Strange, spectacular, and almost unknown to non-locals.

> **Lunch:** **Sainte-Énimie** or **Le Rozier** at the canyon ends — riverside terraces. **Florac** is the earlier fallback.

> **Avoid:** the **Millau viaduct** tourist crush and **Le Puy-en-Velay** city traffic — skirt both; the quiet causses and gorge villages are the whole point.

### ⛺ Overnight — camping near Galamus (Saint-Paul-de-Fenouillet)

- **Camping de l'Agly — Saint-Paul-de-Fenouillet** — small, simple campsite ~5 km from the gorge mouth; the planned base for the dawn ride.
  [Pitchup booking](https://www.pitchup.com/en-us/campsites/france/languedoc-roussillon/pyrenees-orientales/saint_paul_de_fenouillet/camping_de_lagly/) · [gorgesdegalamus.fr camping guide](https://www.gorgesdegalamus.fr/camping/)
- **Camping des Randonneurs — Fenouillet** — wooded park with a view over the village and its two Cathar castles; on-site restaurant and Kota-grill.
  [gorgesdegalamus.fr/camping-des-randonneurs](https://www.gorgesdegalamus.fr/camping-des-randonneurs/)
- **Camping La Tour de France — Latour-de-France (~15 km)** — larger site in Côtes-du-Roussillon wine country if you want more facilities.
  [camping-latourdefrance.fr](https://www.camping-latourdefrance.fr/en/)

## Day 4 · Saturday — Galamus to Belchite

### The gorge at dawn, the Aude canyons & a Pyrenean crossing through the Cerdanya

![Day 4 route — Galamus to Belchite](images/new_day4_galamus_to_belchite.png)

*~481 km · ~7.5 h · scenic mountain crossing, motorway only at the very end · arrive Belchite by afternoon*

Ride the **Galamus balcony road first thing**, before the cars. Then climb the Aude canyons, cross the high border in the **Cerdanya**, and come down the magnificent **N-260** through the Catalan pre-Pyrenees before the final run to Belchite. The scenery does the work; only the last flat stretch to Belchite is motorway.

**Along the road**

1. 💎 **Gorges de Galamus & Ermitage Saint-Antoine** — a 7th-century hermitage clinging to the cliff above the Agly, on a vertiginous one-lane balcony road. **Go at opening time / first light** — it's narrow and best had empty.
2. 💎 **Défilé de Pierre-Lys** — the D117's cliff-cut canyon with rock tunnels below Quillan; tight, dramatic, and a motorcyclist's favourite.
3. 💎 **Gorges de Saint-Georges (Axat)** — reckoned the narrowest road gorge in France, where road and river thread the foot of a 300 m cliff. Spectacular and almost unsigned.
4. 📍 **Ax-les-Thermes → Col de Puymorens** — thermal spa town, then up to the high pass and into the open Cerdagne plateau.
5. 💎 **Llívia** — a Spanish town entirely surrounded by France, an exclave left over from a 1659 treaty; quirky, walkable, with Europe's oldest pharmacy museum.
6. 📍 **Puigcerdà** — Cerdanya's lively old town around its lake; the natural lunch stop before the N-260.
7. 💎 **N-260 "Eix Pirinenc" + the pre-Pyrenees** — one of Europe's great riding roads. Don't rush past **Bellver de Cerdanya** (medieval core), **Organyà** (where the *Homilies* — the oldest known text in Catalan — were found), and **Coll de Nargó** (a perfect Romanesque belfry, Sant Climent, and a dinosaur-egg museum).
8. 📍 **La Seu d'Urgell** — the only Romanesque **cathedral** in Catalonia; medieval arcaded streets.
9. 💎 **Lleida — La Seu Vella** — the dramatic hilltop old cathedral over the plain; a worthy last pause before the run-in to Belchite.

> **Lunch:** **Puigcerdà** (lakeside, before the N-260) or **Bellver de Cerdanya**. Spanish kitchens run late, so eat in the mountains — options thin out on the Aragón plains.

> **Avoid:** **Andorra.** It's tempting next door, but means customs queues, traffic and shopping crowds — the Puigcerdà crossing is cleaner and prettier. And on the final leg, just take the motorway from Lleida to Belchite; the plain is flat and there's nothing to gain on back roads.

### 🏨 Arrival — hotels & hostels in Belchite (no campsites)

Belchite has no campground, so book a room — and it's a small town, so **reserve ahead**, especially for a Saturday night.

- **Hotel Oleum** — modern small hotel in the old Casino building in the centre; 12 rooms incl. suites. The most hotel-like option.
  [hoteloleum.es (official, book direct)](https://hoteloleum.es/)
- **Hostal Restaurante Pueblo Viejo** — simple hostal with its own restaurant and free parking; handy for dinner on arrival.
  [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/es/hostal-restaurante-pueblo-viejo.html)
- **Casa Rural Legus Belikiom** — well-rated guesthouse (≈9.7) at Calle Mayor 56; kitchen, A/C, private parking. ☎ +34 659 828 672.
  [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/es/casa-rural-en-belchite.es.html)
- **Compare all Belchite stays** (incl. Apartahotel Belchite & nearby villages).
  [Booking.com — Belchite](https://www.booking.com/city/es/belchite.html)

> **While you're here:** the ruined **Pueblo Viejo de Belchite** — the old town left as a shell after the 1937 Civil-War battle — is the reason the place is known. Worth the guided visit if you arrive with time.
