ROUTABLE GPX FILES — Uitgeest to Belchite
==========================================

WHAT CHANGED
------------
The earlier GPX files contained only stop waypoints, so navigation apps
drew straight lines between them ("as the crow flies"). These new files
are ROUTE-type GPX (<rte>) with dense shaping points placed along the
actual roads. When you import one, your device/app recalculates it onto
real roads — following the intended D-roads, gorge roads and motorways
rather than cutting straight across country.

HOW TO USE (pick what matches your kit)
---------------------------------------
• Garmin (zumo/Tread), TomTom Rider: copy the .gpx files to the GPX
  folder and import as a ROUTE. The device routes between the shaping
  points automatically. Set profile to "car/motorcycle", NOT "off-road"
  or "straight line" (that setting is what causes crow-flies tracks).

• Calimoto / Scenic / Kurviger apps: Import GPX → choose "as route"
  (not "as track"). The app snaps it to roads and you can re-optimise
  for curves.

• OsmAnd: Import, open the track, then "Plan a route → attach to roads".

ONE-CLICK ALTERNATIVE
---------------------
See ROUTING_LINKS.txt — paste a link into kurviger.de to route in your
browser and export a fresh, fully road-snapped GPX.

NOTE
----
Distances/times quoted in the booklet are estimates from coordinates,
not live routing. Once you import and route these, your device will show
the true road distance and time — check Day 2 (the long one) before you
commit, and use its "faster alternative" if it runs past your window.
